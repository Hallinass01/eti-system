Your horse is now in the ETI review process.

Thank you for submitting your horse’s information, photos, and video. Your case has been captured and organized for ETI review. The goal now is to move from scattered observations into a more structured understanding of what pattern may actually be present.

What Happens Next

1. Your submission is reviewed through the ETI terrain framework.

We look at the horse as a system, not as a list of isolated symptoms.

ETI is designed to help you see the horse more clearly, organize what you are seeing into a real pattern, and begin with direction instead of guessing.

2. Patterns are organized.

Hydration, muscle stress, digestive stability, metabolic strain, and compensation patterns are considered together.

3. Your case is prepared for interpretation.

The goal is to identify what is most likely happening, why it may be happening, and what direction the pattern appears to be moving.

4. You now have a structured starting point.

ETI is designed to move you from confusion to direction.

What Helps the Most

The clearer the submission, the stronger the review. Eye photos, posture images, and short straight-line movement clips are often the most useful for understanding pattern, compensation, and progression.

If more information is needed later, it can be added — but a clear starting submission gives ETI the strongest foundation possible.

Why This Matters

If this were mine, I would want to understand what the horse is actually telling me before the pattern becomes more obvious, more expensive, or harder to correct. That is the purpose of ETI: clearer understanding before deeper consequence.
